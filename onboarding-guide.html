Overview

What's happening

Over the next few weeks, we're building a custom AI assistant that lives inside your Slack workspace and understands your codebase, your team's way of working, and your product.

Think of it as a new team member who has read every line of code in your repositories, knows your documentation, and is available in Slack whenever your team needs them - whether that's writing a feature, creating a pull request, reviewing recent changes, or drafting a document.

Unlike generic AI tools, this agent is built specifically for your environment. It knows your tech stack, your coding conventions, your branch workflow, and your brand voice. It doesn't need to be taught from scratch every session. It remembers.

We handle everything on the technical side. You never need to touch a server, manage infrastructure, or worry about keeping it up to date. We do all of that.

By the end of setup, your team will have a working AI assistant that can be given tasks directly in Slack - and that gets more useful the more you use it.

Rollout plan

What to expect - timeline

Weeks 1-2

Coding agent live in Slack

Your AI assistant is set up, connected to your GitHub, and accessible in Slack. Your engineering team can start giving it tasks: write code, create branches and pull requests, summarise recent commits, answer questions about the codebase.

Weeks 2-4

Automated QA testing if included

The agent is extended to run automated tests against your staging environment - catching regressions before they reach review. It can report results back to Slack and flag failures with context.

Weeks 3-5

Workflow automation if included

We build automations around your recurring operations work: drafting contracts or proposals in your voice, summarising meetings, or handling repetitive document preparation. The exact scope here is tailored to what we found in our discovery conversation.

Each phase begins as soon as the previous one is stable. You'll hear from us at each milestone, and nothing advances without your sign-off.

Access requirements

For your technical lead

These are the technical access credentials that allow the agent to connect to your systems. Your technical lead will know how to find all of these, and we're happy to jump on a quick call to walk through any of them.

1

GitHub Personal Access Token

This allows the agent to read your repositories, create branches, and open pull requests on your team's behalf.

How to create
  1. Go to github.com, click your profile picture, then Settings
  2. In the left sidebar, scroll to Developer settings → Personal access tokens → Fine-grained tokens
  3. Click "Generate new token" and name it Omni Agent
  4. Set expiry to 1 year
  5. Under Permissions, grant: Repository (read and write), Organisation (read), Workflows (read and write)
  6. Click Generate and copy the token

Send via the secure link we provide - not by email.

2

Slack Bot Token

This gives the agent a presence in your Slack workspace. We'll walk you through creating the Slack app together on a short call - it takes about 10 minutes. At the end, you'll have a token starting with xoxb- to send to us.

If you'd prefer to do it yourself, we'll send a step-by-step guide.

3

Staging URL

The full web address of your staging environment, for example: https://staging.yourapp.com

This lets the agent run tests against a real environment without touching production.

4

Tech stack confirmation

A quick written confirmation of your setup:

  • Primary programming language and framework
  • Package manager (npm, yarn, pnpm, etc.)
  • Test runner (Jest, Vitest, Pytest, etc.)
  • Main branch name (main or master)
  • Branch naming convention (e.g. feat/, fix/)
  • Whether pull requests require a reviewer before merging
5

Notion integration if applicable

This allows the agent to read your documentation.

How to set up
  1. Go to notion.so/my-integrations
  2. Click "New integration", name it Omni Agent, and link it to your workspace
  3. Click Submit - you'll see a token starting with secret_
  4. Share your main documentation pages with the integration (open the page, click "..." → Add connections → select Omni Agent)

Send the token via the secure link we provide.

6

Branding assets

Your logo (SVG or high-resolution PNG) and your primary brand colour (hex code, e.g. #1A2B3C). Used to give the agent a consistent visual identity in any outputs it produces.

7

Git workflow confirmation

A brief note confirming how your team works with git. This is usually covered in our discovery call, but written confirmation is helpful.

Access requirements

For your operations lead

These things help us calibrate the agent's tone and give it the context it needs for any communication or document tasks.

1

Gmail app password if email automation included

A special one-time password that lets the agent send emails on your behalf. It's different from your regular Gmail password and can be revoked at any time without changing your account password.

How to create
  1. Go to myaccount.google.com
  2. Click Security → 2-Step Verification (you'll need this enabled)
  3. Scroll to App passwords → click the arrow
  4. Choose "Mail" and "Other device", type Omni Agent, click Generate
  5. Copy the 16-character code (spaces don't matter)

Send via the secure link we provide.

2

Sample documents

Two or three examples of contracts, proposals, or statements of work that your team typically sends to clients. These don't need to be real - anonymised versions are fine, and so is anything you've already sent publicly. PDF or Word format works.

These help us tune the agent so it drafts in your structure and style, not a generic template.

3

Brand voice examples

A handful of emails or Slack messages that feel representative of how your team communicates with clients. No need to prepare anything - just forward us a few examples you like the tone of.

This is the quickest way to help the agent sound like your team rather than a generic AI.

Trust & transparency

Privacy & security

We take this seriously. Here's exactly what we can and can't see.

Credentials are stored in an encrypted system keychain

Not in any file, database, spreadsheet, or cloud storage. Used only to authenticate the agent to your systems.

Your code and data stay on your infrastructure

The agent runs on a server dedicated to your account - either in your own Hetzner Cloud account, or one we provision that is logically isolated from all other clients. We do not copy your codebase to our systems.

We can access your server for maintenance

We have SSH access to your agent's server for the purposes of maintaining, updating, and troubleshooting it. We use this to keep your agent running - not to examine your data.

Tokens can be revoked at any time

If you ever want to end the engagement or rotate credentials, simply revoke the tokens you provided and let us know. We'll update our end accordingly.

We never share your data with third parties

Your credentials, code, and documents are never shared with any third party.

If you have specific compliance requirements - GDPR, SOC2, data residency - let us know and we'll make sure the setup addresses them explicitly.

Common questions

Frequently asked questions

Can the agent push code directly to our main branch?
No. The agent always creates a new branch and opens a pull request. It never pushes directly to your main or master branch. Your team reviews and merges as normal.
What happens if the agent makes a mistake?
The same thing that happens with any developer - you review the PR and request changes, or reject it. The agent can be given feedback directly in Slack ("that approach won't work because...") and it will adjust. Over time, it gets better at understanding your team's standards.
Who can talk to the agent?
Anyone you invite to the agent's Slack channel. You control who has access. We recommend starting with your engineering team and expanding from there once they're comfortable with it.
Does the agent read all of our Slack messages?
No. The agent only reads messages in channels it has been explicitly added to, and direct messages sent to it. It does not have access to your broader Slack workspace history.
What if we want to add a new repository or tool later?
Just let us know. Adding new repositories or integrations is a standard maintenance task. We'll update the agent's configuration and deploy the change - usually within a day.
What if we want to pause or cancel?
You can pause or end the engagement at any time. On your side, you revoke the tokens you've provided. On our side, we shut down the agent's server and delete all associated credentials. There's no lock-in.
How is this different from using ChatGPT or GitHub Copilot?
General AI tools are general - they don't know your codebase, your team's conventions, your staging environment, or your brand voice. They also don't take action: they can't create branches, open PRs, or send emails. Your Omni agent is configured specifically for your environment and can act, not just advise. It also maintains memory across sessions, so it gets more useful over time rather than starting from scratch every conversation.
